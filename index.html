Ballroom Dancing

It started out with me visiting a youth dancing class to meet people but Elena and I both realised that there had to be more than just social dancing. So we seperately decided to visit the follow up advanced course where we met for the first time and became partners. We slowly formed a very close friend group with other pairs and became more and more addicted to the feeling of improvement and showing it all off. Over the next 3 years we visited balls, danced the openings in formations and even choreographed several ball opening dances ourselves to earn a little money on the side - but we mostly did it for the fun of it. The dance floor was our new home, practising almost every day of the week for 2-6 hours at a time - whenever the dancing school was open. We could dance: English Waltz, Tango, Quickstep, Slowfox, Viennese Waltz, Cha Cha, Rumba, Jive, Samba, Paso Doble, Salsa, and Discofox.

Balls

Luckily Elena and I shared the desire to dance on every ball we could and so begun the restless ball seasons. You see the austrian ball season streches roughly from january to may. And as opportunities arose to attend some for free or even perform on the dancefloor - we took them all. Sometimes even going on 3 in a single week, dancing late into the night after a school day or after two dancing lessons on the weekend.

Competitive

After getting the chance to attend a dancing class to improve our technique, we quickly fell in love with the tournament or competitive dancing style which showed us a lot more room for improvement. 1,5 years into extensive training on our own as well as 2 classes per week we decided to try out dancing in a real competition. But the global covid pandemic shut the event down 2 days afore.

Ten Dance

Looking through the many categories a competition had to offer we decided to stick with Ten Dance. It includes the 5 latin and 5 standard dances that we already were familiar with and are commonly seen on a competition's dancefloor.
Ballroom: English Waltz, Tango, Quickstep, Slowfox, Viennese Waltz
Latin: Cha Cha, Rumba, Jive, Samba, Paso Doble.
